About me, Robert Bernard Howell

Short Biography

I was born on November 29 th, 1997 in Camden, New Jersey. I grew up in the small South Jersey town, Woodstown, in Salem county. My high school was small, my graduating class was around 250 students.
At an early age I was always fascinated by machinery and technology, namely how they functioned. I would spend afternoons taking apart old clocks and radios and examining the circuit boards and resistors that were housed inside.
I have created this website to practice my HTML coding skills and learn how to implement CSS into webpages.

My interests

I love to learn, even as a young child I loved to go to school; when my older brother started kindergarten before me I was extremely jealous that I couldn't attend as well.
Where I grew up there was mostly farmland, and most families were farmers or involved in the field of agriculture in some way. This made it extremely hard for me, someone who was deeply interested in STEM, to find good mentors.

Both my parents are environmentalist, but they had little knowledge of engineering,
they would encourage my interests but they were not able to help me pursue my interests in programming and math. The only coding class that my school offered
was a visual basic course, which is not a great introduction to programming because of how little it is used.

My activities

I love to ski, my dad would often take my brother and I on long weekend trips to the Poconos to ski.
I am also a member of the Engineering Frat, Theta Tau, this is where my interest in programming was peaked. I joined a project to develop a website for our branch of the fraternity.
While attending meetings and building the webpage I was assigned, I spent a lot of time with Computer Science and Computer Engineering majors. While talking with them, the courses and coding knowledge they were gaining convinced me to pursue a Masters in Computer Engineering, something I would enjoy more than traditional Mechanical Engineering.
